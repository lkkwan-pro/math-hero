Meeting Minutes: Macroeconomic Outlook, Corporate Strategy, and FX Management

Date: April 26

Participants: Hisense Management Team & Financial Institution Representatives (HSBC)

1. Macroeconomic & Market Outlook

  • China's Economic Performance: First-quarter GDP grew by 5%, primarily driven by resilient export demand and policy-supported investments in infrastructure and manufacturing. However, domestic demand, particularly in consumption and real estate, remains relatively weak.
  • Inflation & Supply Chain: Cost-push inflation is rising due to upstream price increases in oil, gas, and chemicals. Mid-to-downstream manufacturing sectors (like plastics and rubber) have historically low profit margins (under 5%) and may be forced to pass these costs onto consumers.
  • Global Trade & Tariffs: Despite ongoing geopolitical conflicts and US tariffs, China's export sector remains optimistic due to the resilience of its supply chain, which could lead to a reshoring of orders similar to 2021.

2. Hisense Business Updates & Global Strategy

  • Cost & Tariff Pressures: Hisense noted severe impacts from US tariffs and surging raw material costs, particularly plastics, which have increased by nearly 36% since the start of the year. The company struggled to maintain profitability in the US market last year due to naturally thin margins of home appliances.
  • Domestic Market: Recent government trade-in subsidies for consumer goods are viewed as having a limited long-term impact on large home appliances, which typically have a 10-year replacement cycle.
  • International Expansion: Globalization is a core priority, with overseas sales now accounting for nearly 50% of total revenue (approaching 20 billion). Hisense is actively accelerating layout across Latin America, ASEAN, the Middle East, and Africa.

3. Foreign Exchange (FX) & Hedging Strategies

  • RMB Outlook: HSBC forecasts the USD/RMB exchange rate to reach 6.75 by year-end, with short-term fluctuations between 6.7 and 6.9. The PBOC is guiding the RMB toward a steady, slight appreciation.
  • Corporate Hedging Practices: Exporters are managing FX exposure by splitting strategies: 1/3 spot conversions, 1/3 leveraged structured products, and 1/3 utilizing USD/HKD pegged tools (e.g., locking in USD purchases below 7.75).
  • Federal Reserve Policy: Expectations for US rate cuts have cooled (baseline: zero to one cut by end of year/2025), keeping the USD relatively strong.

4. Financing & Hong Kong Market Opportunities

  • Offshore RMB Financing: Hisense is evaluating 3-5 year offshore RMB loans due to exceptionally low interest rates in HK (cheaper than mainland rates). This liquidity window is driven by PBOC and HKMA swap arrangements. HSBC advised locking in these rates now, noting 1-year RMB funding costs can be as low as 1.3% using swap curves.
  • HK as Global Wealth Hub: HK's financial sector is experiencing a strong resurgence, driven by mainland wealth, funds shifting back from Singapore, and Middle Eastern capital seeking a safe service hub.
  • Headquarters Economy: HK is expanding its offshore RMB liquidity pool (currently 1 trillion) and offering tax incentives to encourage multinationals like Hisense to establish offshore management hubs.
  • USD/HKD Peg: HSBC emphasized the Linked Exchange Rate System is structurally unshakeable and protected by the Basic Law. Companies do not need to worry about the peg breaking within the next 5-10 years.
  • Expanded Credit Facilities: Hisense requires expanded limits to fund overseas warehouses. Highlighting zero default risks and reliable trade processes, both risk management teams are communicating to ensure credit ratings reflect Hisense's operational strength beyond raw data.
